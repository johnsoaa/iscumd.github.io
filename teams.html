Team Yeti

Autonomous Snowplow Competition

The ION Autonomous Snowplow Compeition is an annual competition held in late January since 2010. In this competition Student Teams from multiple universities compete to create a snowplow robot that can clear snow both independently and collaborately with antoher robot. The competition hosts teams from half a dozen US States and 2 Canadian Provinces.

Team Yeti is a Founding Club of ASC and was started under the leadership of our Late Advisor Dr. Narasimhamurthi Natarajan known as Nattu, who also lead U of M Dearborn teams in IONs Autonomous Lawnmower Competition which was an annual compeition held in Ohio and help establish the ASC in 2010. At one point UMDearborn was working on 3 seperate Snowplow projects, but we decided it was best to consolidate our skills and resources to focus on optimizing one bot.

Robot

Our bot Yeti is a 360lb bot built on a welded steel frame, powered by 2 12v batteries powering 4 NPC DC Motors. Yeti uses an Ouster 3D lidar and an Intel RealSense tracking camera to collect data to send to the unboard computer which runs a ROS2 software which does pathplanning and object avoidance calculations to allow Yeti to Navigate the competition field.

Team Ohm

Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition

The Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition (IGVC) is hosted by Oakland University in June every year. The competetion is sponsored by the Ground Vehicle Systems Center (GVSC), a division of the US Army that is involved with the development of future combat vehicles. Our team must design a robotic vehicle that can navigate through an outdoor environment, stay within lanes, and avoid obstacles. The robot is judged based on how quickly it can navigate through the course as well as how well the robot is designed.

Robot

Our robot uses a differential drive chassis to navigate through the environment. We use a camera and image detection to determine where the lanes are that our robot must stay within. We use a Lidar sensor to collect a point cloud of the objects around us. Finally, our robot uses ROS, allowing us to utilize ROS navigation methods to determine the most optimal path through the obstacles to reach the goal.

Executive Board

Duty

The Executive Board of ISC is the Leadership of the club, and the bridge between the student engineers and the university. They work to manage club responsibilities, organize club meetings and presentations, secure funding from both the University and outside sponsors, and ensures that the club is running smoothly.
